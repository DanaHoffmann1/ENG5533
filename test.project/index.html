Introduction

My presentation critiques the controversial commonplace of racialized professionalization. I use computer code bootcamps as a case study to explore this approach to teaching in professional and technical communication.


The history of professional and technical communication begins with objectivity. In other words, PTC came out of a need to produce scientific knowledge and ways to use various technology rather than communicating to explore how scientific knowledge and technology perpetuated racial inequality.
